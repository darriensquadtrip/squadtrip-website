– Planning an event today is more complex than it was a few years ago. More people join events remotely, payment expectations have changed, and guests want updates instantly.

– Good event planning tools solve this by keeping everything inside one dashboard.

– You manage RSVPs, track bookings, [create itineraries](/guides/group-trip-itinerary-planning-step-by-step-guide) , [collect forms](/product/collect-registration-information-for-your-group-trip) , and share updates without switching between apps. – A dedicated platform fixes this.

## What to Look For in Event Planning Tools

Before choosing a tool, focus on the features that impact your workflow the most.

### 1\. Easy Booking and Payment Collection

Guests should be able to sign up quickly. Look for clean booking pages, multiple [payment options](https://help.squadtrip.com/en/articles/12291804-what-payment-options-do-trip-organizers-have), and the ability to set [payment plans.](/guides/payment-plans-for-trips)

### 2\. Automated Reminders

Manual follow ups take time. [Payment reminders](https://help.squadtrip.com/en/articles/11100340-how-to-customize-the-payment-reminder-email) , deadline alerts, and schedule updates should be automatic.

### 3\. Customizable Itineraries

Events run better when guests know the plan. Make sure the tool allows clear itinerary pages that are easy to update.

### 4\. Rooming and Accommodation Tools

Retreats and multi day events need room assignments. Not every tool offers this, so check before choosing a platform.

### 5\. Guest Communication

You should be able to send updates without switching to another app. A central communication tool saves time and prevents missed details.

### 6\. Forms and Document Collection

Waivers, ID uploads, questionnaires, and travel details should be collected inside the platform.

### 7\. Mobile Friendly Experience

Most guests complete bookings and check updates on their phones. The tool should work well on mobile.

WeTravel is known for its strong financial features, which is why many travel companies use it as their main payment system. It was built for operators who run tours, retreats, or programs that involve multiple vendors and frequent payouts. The platform makes it easy to collect money, manage installments, and handle supplier payments without juggling spreadsheets or bank transfers.

WeTravel does not try to be a full event management tool. Instead, it focuses on financial workflows. If most of your planning happens outside the platform and you mainly need a reliable payment and payout system, WeTravel fits well.

Hosts and operators who care most about finance, billing, and vendor payments rather than itinerary or guest management tools.

### 3\. Tryp

Tryp is designed for multi day experiences and adventure style trips. It performs best for organizers who want strong trip building tools but do not need deeper event management features like rooming, forms, or detailed guest dashboards. Tryp helps you build a trip visually, outline your schedule, and present it in a clean, easy to understand format for potential guests.

It works well for creators, travel brands, or hosts who want a simple interface to showcase their trip without carrying the weight of a large platform.

#### Key Features

-   Visual trip builder that helps you lay out the experience

Hosts who create travel experiences that need strong itinerary presentation but do not require advanced operational tools.

BookRetreats works as a marketplace that promotes yoga retreats, wellness events, and holistic travel experiences. It gives your retreat instant visibility by placing it in front of thousands of people searching for these types of events. This can help you find new participants without heavy marketing.

#### Key Features

Retreat hosts who want visibility and new leads, especially yoga and wellness instructors who do not mind sharing space with competitors.

### 6\. Cvent

Cvent is built for corporate events and complex conferences. It has tools for registration, venue selection, vendor coordination, and large scale workflows. The platform handles thousands of attendees and offers structured processes that support teams working together.

Cvent is powerful but not lightweight. It can feel overwhelming if you are planning smaller retreats, workshops, or community events.

#### Key Features

-   Corporate registration systems
-   Vendor and venue management tools
-    Badge creation and check in flows
-    Reporting for large teams and organizations

#### Best For

Enterprises, corporate planners, and conference organizers who need advanced control and multiple backend features.

### 7\. Eventbrite

Eventbrite is one of the most popular tools for ticketed events. It shines in situations where a host needs a fast and simple setup. You can create an event, sell tickets, and start promoting in minutes without any learning curve.

Eventbrite is strong for concerts, seminars, parties, and one day workshops. It is not designed for multi day retreats or events with rooming and forms.

#### Key Features

-   Ticket sales with instant setup
-   Event promotion through Eventbrite’s discovery system
-    Simple check in tools
-   Basic communication features

#### Best For

Hosts running single day events, ticketed events, and sessions that do not require deep planning tools.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### 1\. How do I know if my event actually needs planning software instead of spreadsheets?

Most hosts decide to switch when they feel overwhelmed managing guests, payments, and updates manually. If you are constantly fixing broken sheets, chasing payments, or repeating the same information to guests, software becomes the easier option.

### 2\. What problems does an event planning tool solve that Google Sheets never will?

Sheets cannot automate reminders, update schedules in real time, handle payment plans, or store documents under each guest. Once your event has moving parts, sheets turn into extra work instead of helping you stay organized.

### 3\. What features matter most when picking an event planning tool in 2026?

Look for centralized guest info, flexible payment plans, simple itineraries, forms and waivers, automated reminders, vendor tracking, and a clear dashboard. Anything missing from this list will slow you down later.

### 4\. Why do modern event hosts care so much about payment plans?

Because guests expect flexibility. Many people no longer want to pay the full amount upfront. If your tool makes it easy to set installments and send reminders automatically, you increase bookings and reduce follow ups.

### 5\. Is it worth switching tools if I only run a couple of events a year?

Yes, if those events involve multiple guests, payments, forms, or itinerary changes. Even small retreats and workshops run smoother when everything stays in one place instead of multiple apps.
