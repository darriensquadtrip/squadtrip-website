## Beaches in Bali: Where to Relax and Surf

Bali’s coastline varies dramatically depending on where you go. Some beaches are calm and swimmable, while others are better suited for surfing.

### Uluwatu Beaches

Uluwatu is known for dramatic cliffs and world-class surf breaks.

### Padang Padang Beach

Small, scenic, and ideal for photos. Surf conditions can be strong.

### Bingin Beach

Relaxed atmosphere with cliffside views and boutique stays.

### Dreamland Beach

Wide sandy stretch, good for sunbathing and beginner surfers.

Uluwatu works well for group beach days combined with sunset dinners.

### Seminyak Beaches

Seminyak is known for beach clubs and sunset vibes.

-   Double Six Beach
-   Petitenget Beach

This area is perfect for groups who want a mix of beach time and nightlife.

### Canggu Beaches

Canggu attracts digital nomads and younger travelers.

-   Echo Beach
-   Batu Bolong Beach
-   Berawa Beach

Great for surf lessons, café hopping, and relaxed beach days.

If you’re organizing a group surf trip to Bali, plan beginner lessons in advance and schedule flexible mornings. Managing individual payments for activities can get messy.

## Temples to Visit in Bali

No travel guide for Bali is complete without covering its iconic temples. Bali’s spiritual culture is deeply woven into daily life.

### Tanah Lot Temple

Built on a rock formation in the sea, Tanah Lot is one of the most photographed temples in Bali. Visit at sunset for dramatic views.

### Uluwatu Temple

Perched on a cliff, Uluwatu Temple offers sweeping ocean views. The Kecak fire dance at sunset is a popular cultural experience.

### Besakih Temple

Known as the “Mother Temple,” Besakih sits on the slopes of Mount Agung and is the largest temple complex in Bali.

### Tirta Empul Temple

Famous for its purification ritual pools. Visitors can participate respectfully.

### Temple Etiquette Tips

-   Wear a sarong and sash.
-   Shoulders and knees must be covered.
-   Avoid visiting during certain ceremonies unless invited.
-   Women on their menstrual cycle traditionally do not enter temples.

For group temple visits, assign a fixed meeting point and time. Large crowds are common during peak season.

## Ubud: Culture, Rice Terraces, and Waterfalls

Ubud is Bali’s cultural heart. If your group prefers wellness, art, and nature, Ubud should be a priority.

### Tegalalang Rice Terraces

Iconic layered green fields. Visit early morning to avoid crowds.

### Sacred Monkey Forest

Home to hundreds of macaques. Secure your belongings carefully.

### Tegenungan Waterfall

Easily accessible and popular for swimming and photos.

### Yoga and Wellness Retreats

Ubud is a global hub for yoga and meditation retreats. Many villas offer private group sessions.

## Best Food to Try in Bali

Bali’s food scene blends traditional Indonesian dishes with global cuisine.

### Traditional Balinese Dishes

#### Nasi Goreng

Indonesian fried rice served with egg and sambal.

#### Mie Goreng

Fried noodles with vegetables and protein.

#### Babi Guling

Balinese roast pork, typically served during ceremonies.

#### Satay

Grilled skewers served with peanut sauce.

## Where to Eat in Bali

### Warungs

Local, affordable, authentic meals.

### Beach Clubs

Laid-back daytime dining with ocean views.

### Fine Dining

Bali has multiple globally recognized restaurants offering tasting menus.

For group dinners, pre-book reservations and agree on budget ranges. Splitting bills across 10–20 people can become complicated without structure.

## Best Time to Visit Bali

Bali has two main seasons:

### Dry Season (May to September)

-   Sunny days
-   Lower humidity
-   Peak tourism
-   Higher accommodation prices

### Rainy Season (October to April)

-   Short afternoon showers
-   Fewer crowds
-   Lower prices
-   Lush landscapes

For group trips, May, June, and September offer the best balance of weather and price.

## Budgeting for a Bali Trip

Costs vary depending on travel style.

### Accommodation

-   Budget hostel: $10–20 per night
-   Mid-range hotel: $50–120
-   Private villa: $200+ per night

### Food

-   Local meals: $3–8

## Frequently Asked Questions About Bali

### 1\. What is the best time to visit Bali?

The best time to visit Bali is during the dry season from May to September. These months offer sunny weather, lower humidity, and ideal beach conditions. May, June, and September are especially good because they balance great weather with slightly lower prices and fewer crowds compared to peak July and August.

### 2\. Is Bali safe for tourists?

Yes, Bali is generally safe for tourists. Petty theft can happen in busy areas, so keep valuables secure and avoid leaving belongings unattended at beaches or cafés. Use reputable transportation providers and exercise normal travel precautions.

### 3\. How many days do you need in Bali?

Five to seven days is ideal for first-time visitors. This allows enough time to explore beaches in Uluwatu or Seminyak, experience Ubud’s rice terraces and temples, and enjoy local food. If you want a slower pace or plan island hopping, consider staying 8 to 10 days.

### 4\. Which area is best to stay in Bali?

**It depends on your travel style:**

-   Canggu is great for digital nomads, cafés, and surf culture.  
    
-   Seminyak is ideal for beach clubs and nightlife.  
    
-   Uluwatu is perfect for dramatic cliffs and surf beaches.  
    
-   Ubud is best for culture, wellness, and jungle scenery.  
    

### 5\. Is Bali expensive?

Bali can be very affordable or quite luxurious depending on your choices. Budget travelers can spend as little as $30–50 per day, while luxury travelers staying in private villas and dining at high-end restaurants may spend $200+ per day.
