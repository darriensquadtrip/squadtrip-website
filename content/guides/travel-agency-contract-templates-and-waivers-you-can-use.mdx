## What Should a Travel Agency Contract Include?

A travel agency contract outlines the business terms between the organizer and the traveler. Below are the core sections every template should include.

### 1\. Trip Details and Scope of Services

**Clearly define:**

-   Destination  
    
-   Dates  
    
-   Inclusions  
    
-   Exclusions  
    
-   Accommodation type  
    
-   Transportation details  
    
-   Activities included  
    

Avoid vague language like “luxury stay” or “private transport.” Be specific.

**Example:**

-   4-star hotel, double occupancy  
    
-   Round-trip airport transfers  
    
-   3 guided excursions  
    

Specific language prevents refund disputes later.

### 2\. Payment Terms and Installment Schedule

**This section should include:**

-   Total trip cost  
    
-   Deposit amount  
    
-   Payment deadlines  
    
-   Accepted payment methods  
    
-   Late payment penalties  
    

If you allow installment plans, define what happens if someone misses a payment.

Clear payment structures reduce back-and-forth emails and protect your cash flow.

### 3\. Cancellation and Refund Policy

This is one of the most important sections in Travel Agency Contract Templates and Waivers.

**Include:**

-   Refund timeline (e.g., 90 days, 60 days, 30 days before departure)  
    
-   Non-refundable deposits  
    
-   Partial refund scenarios  
    
-   Trip cancellation by organizer terms  
    
-   Force majeure clause  
    

Avoid overly complicated language. Keep it direct and structured.

**For example:**

-   90+ days: Full refund minus deposit  
    
-   60–89 days: 50% refund  
    
-   Under 60 days: No refund  
    

Clarity here prevents emotional disputes later.

### 4\. Liability Disclaimer

**A waiver section limits your liability for:**

-   Airline delays  
    
-   Weather disruptions  
    
-   Third-party vendors  
    
-   Injuries during activities  
    
-   Lost luggage  
    

You cannot eliminate all risk, but you can define responsibility boundaries.

Include language that clarifies you act as an intermediary between travelers and service providers.

### 5\. Assumption of Risk

**If your trips involve:**

-   Hiking  
    
-   Adventure sports  
    
-   International travel  
    
-   Remote destinations  
    
-   Water activities  
    

You need a clear assumption-of-risk clause.

This confirms that participants understand and accept the inherent risks involved in travel and activities.

### 6\. Medical Disclosure and Emergency Contacts

For group trips, this section is critical.

**Collect:**

-   Dietary restrictions  
    
-   Allergies  
    
-   Medical conditions  
    
-   Emergency contact information  
    
-   Required medications  
    

You cannot plan properly without this data.

### 7\. Code of Conduct

**Define expected behavior:**

-   Respectful conduct  
    
-   Alcohol policies  
    
-   Drug policies  
    
-   Harassment rules  
    
-   Grounds for removal  
    

This protects the group experience.

Include a clause stating that violation of conduct rules may result in removal without refund.

### 8\. Image and Media Release

If you take photos or videos during the trip:

Include a media release clause allowing you to use images for marketing purposes.

Offer an opt-out option if needed.

### 9\. Governing Law and Jurisdiction

State which country or state laws apply and where disputes will be handled.

This is especially important for international trips.

## What Is a Travel Waiver?

A waiver is a legal document where travelers agree not to hold you liable for certain risks.

While contracts define business terms, waivers focus on liability protection.

They are often combined into a single document but serve different legal functions.

### Types of Travel Agency Waivers You May Need

Not all trips require the same waiver. Here are common types.

#### General Liability Waiver

**Covers:**

-   Minor injuries  
    
-   Property damage  
    
-   Accidents during planned activities  
    

Standard for most group trips.

#### Adventure Activity Waiver

**Required for:**

-   Scuba diving  
    
-   Trekking  
    
-   Mountain climbing  
    
-   Skiing  
    
-   Safari tours  
    

Higher-risk activities require more detailed waivers.

#### International Travel Waiver

**Addresses:**

-   Visa issues  
    
-   Political instability  
    
-   Health risks  
    
-   Travel advisories  
    

Especially important for multi-country tours.

#### COVID or Health-Related Waiver

Some agencies still include clauses addressing communicable disease risks.

This clarifies that exposure risks cannot be fully controlled.

## How to Structure Travel Agency Contract Templates and Waivers

When creating templates, follow these principles:

Avoid overly legal jargon. Travelers should understand what they are signing.

### Use Headings

**Break documents into sections with clear titles:**

-   Payments  
    
-   Cancellations  
    
-   Liability

**Ensure your:**

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What is included in travel agency contract templates?

Travel agency contract templates typically include trip details, payment terms, cancellation and refund policies, liability disclaimers, assumption of risk clauses, medical disclosures, code of conduct, and governing law sections. Well-structured contracts clearly define what is included in the trip and what is not, helping prevent disputes later.

### Are travel waivers legally enforceable?

In most jurisdictions, travel waivers are legally enforceable if they are clearly written, voluntarily signed, and not misleading. They must not attempt to waive liability for gross negligence or illegal conduct. It’s always recommended to have your waiver reviewed by a legal professional.

### What is the difference between a travel contract and a travel waiver?

A travel contract defines the business agreement between the agency and the traveler, including payments, cancellations, and services provided. A waiver focuses specifically on liability protection and confirms that the traveler understands and accepts certain risks associated with the trip.

### Do small travel agencies need contracts and waivers?

Yes. Even small or new agencies need structured travel agency contract templates and waivers. One dispute, injury, or chargeback can significantly impact a small business. Contracts protect both your revenue and your reputation.

### Can I use a free travel contract template online?

You can start with a template, but free templates often lack jurisdiction-specific language or risk coverage for your particular trip type. It’s best to customize templates to your business and have them reviewed by a legal professional.
