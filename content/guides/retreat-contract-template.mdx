**Introduction**

Planning a retreat involves much more than choosing a location and designing sessions. One of the most overlooked yet important pieces is the Retreat Contract Template you use with your guests. Within the first few days of promotion, questions about refunds, cancellations, responsibilities, and expectations usually start coming in.

A solid retreat contract template sets boundaries early, prevents confusion later, and protects both you and your guests if plans change. This guide breaks down what to include in guest agreements, how detailed they should be, and how to use them without creating friction in the booking process.

If you [host wellness retreats](/guides/host-a-wellness-retreat/), [group trips](/guides/how-to-plan-a-group-trip-without-fear/), coaching intensives, or multi-day experiences, this is a must-have foundation.

## Why a Retreat Contract Template Matters

Retreats are personal experiences. Guests are investing time, money, and trust. Without a written agreement, misunderstandings can escalate quickly.

**A retreat contract template helps you:**

-   Clearly define what guests are paying for
-   Set expectations around schedules and changes
-   Protect yourself from liability issues
-   Avoid refund disputes
-   Reduce back-and-forth emails

Most issues hosts face are not about bad intent. They come from unclear assumptions. A written agreement keeps everyone aligned.

## What a Retreat Contract Is (and What It Is Not)

A retreat contract is not a 10-page legal document filled with dense language. It is a practical agreement that outlines the basics of participation.

**It should:**

-   Be easy to read
-   Use plain language
-   Match how you actually run your retreat
-   Be shared before or at booking

**It should not:**

-   Overpromise outcomes
-   Include vague or open-ended terms
-   Conflict with your marketing page

Your retreat contract template should support trust, not intimidate guests.

## Key Sections Every Retreat Contract Template Should Include

Below are the essential sections you should include in your guest agreement, regardless of retreat type.

### Retreat Overview and Description

Start with a short, clear description of the retreat.

**Include:**

-   Retreat name
-   Dates
-   Location
-   Type of experience ([wellness](/guides/how-to-host-a-retreat-guide/), [coaching](/guides/sports-retreat-ideas-for-coaches-and-active-retreat-hosts/), adventure, [creative](/guides/planning-a-creative-retreat-a-complete-guide-for-hosts-and-coaches/), etc.)

This anchors the agreement to a specific event and avoids confusion if you host multiple retreats.

### Payment Terms and Pricing

This is one of the most important sections of your retreat contract template.

**Cover:**

-   Total cost of the retreat
-   Deposit amount (if applicable)
-   [Payment schedule](https://help.squadtrip.com/en/articles/10758207-how-to-build-a-custom-payment-schedule)
-   Accepted payment methods
-   Currency

Be specific. If payments are split or staged, list dates clearly.

Avoid phrases like “payment due before retreat” without dates.

### Cancellation and Refund Policy

This section prevents the majority of disputes.

**Clearly outline:**

-   Guest cancellation deadlines
-   Refund eligibility by date
-   Non-refundable deposits
-   What happens if a guest leaves early
-   Policy for no-shows

**Example structure:**

-   Cancellation before X date: partial refund
-   Cancellation after X date: no refund

Avoid emotional language. Keep it factual and consistent with what you can realistically honor.

### Host Cancellation or Changes

Your retreat contract template should also explain what happens if you need to cancel or modify the retreat.

**Include:**

-   Minimum guest numbers required
-   Conditions under which the retreat may be canceled
-   Refund or credit process if the host cancels
-   Right to make reasonable schedule changes

Guests appreciate transparency here. It builds trust rather than fear.

### Travel and Accommodation Responsibilities

Clarify what is included and what is not.

**State clearly:**

-   Whether flights are included or excluded
-   Check-in and check-out expectations
-   Room sharing rules (if applicable)
-   Transportation details

Many issues arise when guests assume things that were never promised.

### Health, Fitness, and Medical Disclaimer

This section is critical for wellness, [yoga](/guides/how-to-plan-a-yoga-retreat-from-vision-to-itinerary/), adventure, and [fitness retreats.](/guides/fitness-retreats-training-recovery-guide/)

**Include language that states:**

-   Guests are responsible for assessing their own fitness and health
-   Medical conditions should be disclosed if relevant
-   Activities are voluntary
-   The retreat is not medical treatment

This protects you and helps guests make informed decisions.

## Liability Waiver and Assumption of Risk

Your retreat contract template should include a basic liability waiver.

**This typically covers:**

-   Assumption of risk for activities
-   Release of liability for injuries or losses
-   Agreement to follow safety instructions

This does not remove all risk, but it shows clear intent and awareness.

## Code of Conduct and Behavior Expectations

Set behavioral expectations upfront.

**Include:**

-   Respectful conduct toward others
-   Zero tolerance for harassment or abuse
-   Substance use rules (if applicable)
-   Right to remove guests who violate policies

This helps maintain a safe environment for everyone.

## Photography, Video, and Media Use

If you plan to take photos or videos, address it in the contract.

**Clarify:**

-   Whether media may be used for marketing
-   How guests can opt out
-   Privacy considerations

Transparency here avoids awkward conversations later.

## Force Majeure Clause

This covers events beyond anyone’s control.

**Examples include:**

-   Natural disasters
-   Government restrictions
-   Travel bans
-   Public health emergencies

State how refunds or credits will be handled in such cases.

## Governing Law and Jurisdiction

Include which country or state laws apply to the agreement.

This is especially important for international retreats.

Keep this section simple and factual.

## Signature and Acceptance

**Your retreat contract template should include:**

-   Guest name
-   Signature or digital acceptance
-   Date

Digital acceptance is usually sufficient for most retreats.

## How Detailed Should a Retreat Contract Be?

More detail is not always better.

**Aim for:**

-   Clear policies
-   Short paragraphs
-   Simple wording
-   Logical structure

If guests need a lawyer to understand your contract, it is too complex.

The goal is clarity, not intimidation.

## Common Mistakes Hosts Make With Retreat Contracts

**Avoid these common issues:**

-   Copying contracts that do not match your retreat
-   Using vague refund language
-   Forgetting to update dates or locations
-   Sending the contract after payment
-   Not enforcing the terms consistently

Your retreat contract template should evolve as you learn from each experience.

## When to Share the Retreat Contract With Guests

**Best practice is to share the contract:**

-   Before payment
-   During the booking process
-   As part of confirmation emails

Guests should never feel surprised by policies after they have paid.

## Sample Retreat Contract Template Structure

**Here is a simple structure you can adapt:**

1.  Retreat overview
2.  Payment terms
3.  Cancellation and refunds
4.  Host changes
5.  Travel and accommodation
6.  Health and liability
7.  Code of conduct
8.  Media consent
9.  Force majeure
10.  Governing law
11.  Acceptance

This structure works for most retreat types and can be customized.

## Final Checklist Before Using Your Retreat Contract Template

**Before sending your contract to guests, confirm:**

-   Policies match your sales page
-   Dates and prices are accurate
-   Language is easy to understand
-   You are comfortable enforcing each clause

If something feels unrealistic, revise it.

## FAQs

### 1\. Is a retreat contract legally required for hosting a retreat?

A retreat contract is not always legally required, but it is strongly recommended. It helps set clear expectations and reduces disputes around payments, cancellations, and responsibilities.

### 2\. Can I use the same retreat contract template for different retreats?

You can reuse a base template, but you should update dates, locations, pricing, and policies for each retreat to keep the agreement accurate.

### 3\. When should guests sign or accept the retreat contract?

Guests should accept the retreat contract before or at the time of booking, ideally during the payment process.

### 4\. What happens if a guest cancels after signing the retreat contract?

The cancellation terms in the contract apply. This usually means refunds depend on the cancellation timeline stated in the agreement.

### 5\. Do I need a liability waiver in my retreat contract template?

Yes. A liability waiver helps protect the host by clearly stating that guests understand and accept the risks involved in retreat activities.
