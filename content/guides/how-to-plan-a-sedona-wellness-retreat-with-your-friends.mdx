## FAQs

### 1\. How much does it cost to plan a Sedona wellness retreat with friends?

The cost depends on accommodation style, activities, and group size. On average, expect $900 to $2,000 per person for a 3 to 4 day retreat including lodging, food, and guided experiences. Setting clear payment deadlines and installment options makes budgeting easier for everyone.

### 2\. What is the best time of year to plan a Sedona wellness retreat?

The most popular seasons are spring (March to May) and fall (September to November) because of comfortable temperatures and ideal hiking weather. Summer can be hot, while winter is quieter and more affordable.

### 3\. How many people should attend a friends-based wellness retreat?

A group of 6 to 12 people works well for shared vacation homes and group sessions. Smaller groups create a more intimate experience, while larger groups require more coordination and structured planning.

### 4\. Do I need permits for hiking or group activities in Sedona?

Most hiking trails do not require special permits, but parking passes such as a Red Rock Pass may be required. If hiring local facilitators or hosting larger public retreats, check local guidelines and property rules.

### 5\. How do I handle group travel payments without awkward conversations?

Set clear pricing, deposit amounts, and deadlines upfront. Instead of using spreadsheets or informal payment apps, use a dedicated group travel platform like SquadTrip to collect deposits, schedule installments, and automate reminders.
