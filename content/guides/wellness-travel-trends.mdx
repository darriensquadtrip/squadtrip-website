This article is an overview of 13 global wellness travel trends in 2025 that you may want to know:

### **1.  Family Retreats – Reconnecting with Children and Partner**

A healthy family means a healthy home. Many young parents now prefer subscribing to travel plans that offer educational tours and other programs for the physical and mental well-being of their children. Instead of roaming free, family retreats offer travel experiences to learn, create, and thrive together in a healthy environment surrounded by beautiful scenes of nature. 

Furthermore, in places like New York, family wellness retreats are increasingly popular, offering both countryside escapes and urban wellness experiences that focus on mindfulness, outdoor activities, and holistic health for parents and children alike.

### **2\. Wellness Retreat to Deal with Stress and Burnout**

The hectic lifestyle and redundant work routine often lead to stress and burnout. Many individuals find wellness experiences the best way to rewind, feel the six senses, and be mindful. 

Meditation instructors offer sessions related to stress relief techniques, surrounded by nature that provide indulgence, peace, and tranquility to mind, body, and soul. This helps individuals to reset and recover from the emotional and physical exhaustion of daily urban life. 

There are dozens of travel packages for burnout retreats on travel platforms. It helps instructors understand client preferences and create robust itineraries that deal with stress and burnout. 

### **3\. Women-led Retreats for Healing and Support**

Women’s empowerment comes from shared healing. Wellness retreats for women are a popular choice because they offer a safe and supportive space for participants to heal, share their stories, and connect with other like-minded women. 

The itineraries are closely designed by experienced wellness coaches to include sessions about nurturing, rejuvenation, and healing, empowering women to return to their daily lives with renewed strength and purpose. Many retreats also offer specialized programs for women experiencing menopause, providing resources and support for navigating this life stage with holistic approaches to emotional and physical well-being.

These activities offer women a space to heal from male-centric environments and build meaningful connections with other women. You can find many upcoming women-led retreats on SquadTrip.

### **4\. Combining Wellness Retreat with Art**

You might have seen art workshops for meditation. A recent rise in art therapy with wellness retreats has become a center of attention on social media, especially in Europe. Retreat planners now customize Itineraries with a unique blend of creativity and self-care such as painting, drawing, or crafting, with wellness offerings like yoga, meditation, and mindfulness. 

This gives travelers an opportunity to breathe in the enchanting scenes of nature, explore their inner creativity, and bring them out on an empty canvas with no judgment or stress. 

### **5\. Cultural and Heritage Retreat**

Although this type of travel has always existed. Many people are curious about the histories of healing and mindfulness by different cultures worldwide. These retreats often highlight the intersection of cultural preservation and wellness, combined with mindfulness, connection to nature, and community involvement.

Ideal destinations for cultural and heritage retreats on SquadTrip include places like **Bali**, known for its traditions and artisan culture, or **India**, where yoga and Ayurveda are deeply intertwined with the country’s heritage. **Japan** is also a popular destination where wellness resorts and saunas are common places to enjoy meditation and healing.  

### **6\. Spiritual Wellness and Healing** 

Spiritual healing through wellness retreats is becoming a powerful way for individuals to address emotional and mental well-being. For example, **Thailand** is an ideal destination where practitioners often combine traditional Thai practices like Vipassana meditation at temples and monasteries to provide holistic views filled with healing and rejuvenation. 

As the wellness economy grows, more travelers are seeking these retreats to not only relax but also invest in their overall mental and emotional health, contributing to the rising demand for such transformative experiences worldwide.

### **7\. Night Meditation Retreats for Insomnia-stricken Individuals**

Adults with chronic insomnia are now signing up for retreats to learn and develop meditation habits. Meditation practitioners often organize trips focused on activities like yoga, spa treatments, workshops, and other activities to teach sleep meditation. They choose locations that offer nature and quietness to the mind for authentic experiences.

Since this type of meditation requires extra detailed itineraries, platforms like **SquadTrip** provide instructors the ability to design plans for each day to help attendees develop healthy sleeping habits and recover from insomnia without being dependent on sleeping pills. 

### **8\. Crochet/Knitting Holiday Trips**

Crochet/Knitting became a popular hobby among young adults during the pandemic when everything was closed off. Now, wellness retreats are bringing together crocheters and knitters to scenic locations where they can explore creativity, relaxation, and social connection.

These retreats are designed for both beginners and experienced crafters, providing workshops, guided projects, and opportunities to learn new techniques. Hosts also share their patterns and interesting skill workshops for attendees to learn from. Most popular trips include Christmas Crochet Retreats, Crochet Weekend Retreats, etc.

### **9\. Mental Health Awareness Retreat**

You might have heard of the phrase: “_Nature Heals Everything_.” Addressing mental health issues during healing trips surrounded by nature can be a great experience. Instead of sitting in a quiet space for an hour, these trips offer sessions and workshops focused on body and mind therapies while witnessing enchanting spots. 

Psychologists and wellness coaches usually work together to design sessions that offer techniques to calm the nervous system through somatic experiences and auditory sounds of nature. 

### **10\. Outdoor Adventure with Yoga**

Many yoga instructors are now combining outdoor activities with yoga sessions to balance the physical and mental well-being of the participants. Trips are now designed with more adventurous outdoor activities informed by local culture and tradition, allowing travelers to reconnect with nature and the wild.

This trend aligns with the growing focus on wellness within the tourism industry, as travelers increasingly seek experiences that nurture both their physical health and emotional well-being.
