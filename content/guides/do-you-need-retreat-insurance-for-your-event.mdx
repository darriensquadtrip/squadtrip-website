## Top Tips for Retreat Insurance

Planning a retreat takes more than great programming and a scenic venue – it takes protection. One overlooked but critical step in your planning journey is securing retreat insurance.

Whether you’re a wellness coach hosting a yoga retreat in Bali or a leadership facilitator planning a weekend in the mountains, retreat insurance helps cover your bases. It’s not just about legal coverage – it’s about peace of mind.

## Why This Matters for Retreat Hosts

### It Only Takes One Thing to Go Wrong

Imagine a guest trips during a hike or gets food poisoning from a caterer you hired. These things happen – and without proper insurance, you could be liable.

Many retreat hosts are surprised to learn that:

-   Venue coverage usually doesn’t include third-party liability.
-   Health and safety laws vary by location.
-   One claim can derail your entire retreat business.

Retreat insurance is not just a nice-to-have. It’s a foundational tool for sustainable hosting.

### Key Benefits of Retreat Insurance

Here’s what the right coverage helps you do:

-   Protect Your Business Assets: Safeguard against lawsuits, cancellations, and vendor disputes.
-   Build Trust With Guests: Show professionalism by offering coverage and explaining safety policies.
-   Host With Confidence: Focus on transformation, not logistics or worst-case scenarios.
-   Secure High-Quality Venues: Many require proof of liability insurance before booking.

### Common Mistakes to Avoid

Many first-time hosts learn the hard way. Avoid these common missteps:

-   Assuming you’re covered by the venue – you likely aren’t.
-   Not disclosing risky activities – like paddleboarding or guided hikes.
-   Skipping insurance for small retreats – even a 5-person event can go wrong.
-   Delaying purchase – event cancellation insurance is time-sensitive.

## Understanding the Types of Retreat Insurance You May Need

When selecting retreat insurance, it’s essential to match your coverage to your specific event type. A yoga retreat in the U.S. has very different needs than a culinary retreat in other countries.

### General Event Insurance and General Liability

Most hosts will need general liability insurance to protect against claims related to bodily injury, property damage, or medical expenses – but that’s just the start.

### Look Into Event Liability Insurance

Depending on your offerings, you may also need event liability insurance, host liquor liability if serving alcohol, or special event insurance for one-time gatherings. Review your coverage limits carefully and ensure your policy will provide coverage for venue requirements, supplier contracts, and any event helper you hire.

Without the right liability insurance, you could be held personally responsible if something goes wrong. Protect your retreat business, your guests, and your peace of mind by building a plan that covers every angle.

### Do You Need Special Event Insurance?

Special event insurance typically includes coverage for cancellations, property damage, and liability – offering a safety net if weather, venue problems, or accidents disrupt your plans.

## Practical Tips and Strategies

### Step-by-Step: How to Get Retreat Insurance

1.  Know Your Retreat Type  
    Are you hosting a yoga retreat, business mastermind, or silent meditation retreat? The activities determine your risk.
2.  Outline the Risks  
    Consider location, transportation, food, outdoor activities, and physical exertion.
3.  Choose Your Coverage  
    Typical policies include:
    
    -   General liability insurance
    -   Professional liability insurance
    -   Event cancellation coverage
    -   Travel insurance for guests (optional, but smart)
4.  Find a Specialized Provider  
    Use a broker that understands retreats, like BeYogi, ACT Insurance, or a local commercial agent.
5.  Compare Policies  
    Don’t just look at cost – review deductibles, exclusions, and cancellation terms.
6.  Provide Proof to Venues and Vendors  
    Once secured, share your Certificate of Insurance (COI) as needed.

### What to Ask When Comparing Retreat Insurance

-   What is the per-incident and aggregate coverage limit?
-   Is coverage valid in my retreat location (especially for international retreats)?
-   Are subcontractors or guest facilitators included?
-   Is participant injury covered?
-   Are refunds covered if I cancel due to illness or disaster?
