## Step 1: Create Structured Pricing for Your Ayurveda Retreat

Before thinking about software, you need a clear pricing model.

### Define What’s Included

**Be specific about:**

-   Accommodation type (shared vs private)  
    
-   Daily Ayurvedic treatments  
    
-   Meals (sattvic, detox, personalized)  
    
-   Workshops and consultations  
    
-   Transportation (if applicable)  
    

When pricing is vague, payment confusion increases.

### Offer Tiered Pricing

**Many Ayurveda retreats benefit from:**

-   Early bird pricing  
    
-   Standard pricing  
    
-   VIP/private room upgrades  
    

This increases conversions while giving guests flexible options.

### Build Installment Plans

Ayurveda retreats are premium experiences. Allowing guests to pay in installments makes your retreat more accessible.

**Instead of:**

Pay $3,000 upfront

**Offer:**

-   Deposit to secure spot  
    
-   Monthly installment schedule  
    
-   Final payment due before retreat  
    

This single decision can significantly increase bookings.

## FAQs: Organizing Payments and Guests for an Ayurveda Retreat

### What is the best way to collect payments for an Ayurveda retreat?

The best way to collect payments for an Ayurveda retreat is through a structured booking platform that supports deposits and installment plans. Since retreats are often high-ticket experiences, offering flexible payment schedules increases conversions while keeping everything organized in one place.

### Should I offer installment plans for my Ayurveda retreat?

Yes. Installment plans make your Ayurveda retreat more accessible and reduce the barrier of upfront payment. Most hosts collect a deposit to secure the spot and then divide the remaining balance into monthly payments leading up to the retreat.

### How much deposit should I charge for an Ayurveda retreat?

A common approach is to charge 20–40% of the total retreat price as a non-refundable deposit. This confirms commitment while giving guests time to pay the remaining balance in installments.

### How do I track who has paid and who hasn’t?

Using a centralized dashboard is the most efficient way to track payment status. Instead of spreadsheets, a dedicated group booking system allows you to see deposits paid, installment progress, overdue balances, and total collected revenue in real time.

### What guest information should I collect for an Ayurveda retreat?

**You should collect:**

-   Full name and contact details  
    
-   Dietary preferences  
    
-   Health conditions or allergies  
    
-   Emergency contact information  
    
-   Room preference  
    
-   Arrival and departure details  
    

Collecting this information through structured booking forms ensures accuracy and avoids scattered data.
