## What is Tourwriter?

Tourwriter is an [itinerary planning](/guidesbest-travel-itinerary-builders-for-planning-stress-free-trips) and management system used by travel agencies around the world. It helps businesses create detailed travel plans, vendor quotes, templates, and proposals. Many small and mid-sized travel businesses rely on it to manage itineraries, supplier rates, and customized proposals.

### Strengths of Tourwriter

-   Strong itinerary design tools  
    
-   Centralized vendor database  
    
-   Pricing, costing, and quoting capabilities  
    
-   Proposal generation with customizable layouts  
    

### Limitations for Group Travel

**Tourwriter was originally built for bespoke travel designers and may not be optimized for:**

-   Scaling group logistics  
    
-   Real-time team communication  
    
-   Tracking multiple participants’ preferences  
    
-   Coordinating across numerous suppliers simultaneously  
    

This is where a Tourwriter alternative for group tour operators like SquadTrip comes into play.

## What is SquadTrip?

SquadTrip is a tour operations platform focused on group travel. It brings together planning, team coordination, booking management, communication, and progress tracking in one place. The goal is simple: give group travel teams a clear, collaborative tool that reduces manual work and miscommunication.

Rather than focusing just on itinerary design like some platforms, SquadTrip is built to [manage group tour](/guideshow-to-plan-a-group-trip) workflows from start to finish.

### Core Concepts Behind SquadTrip

-   Group-first design: Every feature assumes you’re managing multiple people, not just a single itinerary.  
    
-   Team collaboration: Shared workspaces, comments, and notifications help teams stay aligned.  
    
-   Task automation: Routine steps are automated, freeing up time to focus on creative planning and customer experience.  
    
-   Integrated communication: Messaging and alerts reduce the need for external chat tools.

## FAQs

### 1\. Is SquadTrip a full replacement for Tourwriter or do they solve different problems?

They overlap, but the focus is different. Tourwriter shines at bespoke itinerary design and quoting. SquadTrip is built for managing group tours end to end, including participants, tasks, communication, and internal workflows. If group logistics matter more than proposal polish, SquadTrip covers more ground.

### 2\. Why do group tour operators switch from Tourwriter to SquadTrip?

Most switches happen when teams grow. Operators hit limits with coordination, task tracking, and real-time updates in Tourwriter. SquadTrip removes the need for separate tools like spreadsheets, Slack, or email threads by handling group workflows in one place.

### 3\. Does SquadTrip handle complex group details like rooming, dietary needs, and preferences?

Yes. SquadTrip is designed around group participant data. Rooming rules, dietary requirements, accessibility notes, and payment status live in one shared view so teams are not chasing details across files.

### 4\. Can Tourwriter manage group bookings at scale?

It can, but it is not built specifically for that use case. Many group operators end up layering manual processes on top of Tourwriter to track participants, tasks, and updates. SquadTrip removes that manual layer by design.

### 5\. How does team collaboration differ between SquadTrip and Tourwriter?

Tourwriter does not include built-in messaging or task-based collaboration. SquadTrip keeps conversations, comments, and updates tied directly to tasks and bookings so context is never lost.
