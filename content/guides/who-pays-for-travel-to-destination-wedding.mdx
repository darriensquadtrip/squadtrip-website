## **Understanding** **Destination Wedding Etiquette****: Who Typically Pays?**

When planning a destination wedding, knowing who covers the costs is crucial. This helps in managing budgets and setting clear expectations for both the couple, you as a planner, and their guests.

Here is a breakdown of the division of responsibilities in a destination wedding:

### **The Couple and Their Families**

Typically, the couple and their family members handle most of the wedding expenses. For a wedding party in Italy, the couple might hire a local florist, while the parents cover the welcome dinner, creating a balanced and memorable experience.

A common share of duties includes:

-   **Venue and Reception:** The couple usually pays for the venue, catering, and other reception-related costs like entertainment.
-   **Ceremony and Décor:** Expenses for the ceremony, floral arrangements, and overall décor are also covered by the couple.
-   **Rehearsal Dinner****:** Often, the groom’s family covers the rehearsal dinner or pre-wedding, but sometimes both families contribute.

### **The Guests**

Wedding guests are generally responsible for their own travel and accommodation costs which include:

-   **Airfare** **and** **Hotel Rooms****:** Guests book and pay for their own airfare and lodging.
-   **Additional Expenses:** Meals, shopping, and other incidentals are also covered by guests.
-   **Attire and Grooming:** Bridesmaids pay for their dresses and hair styling whereas groomsmen cover their tuxedos.

## **Tips for Managing** **Travel Costs** **Effectively**

Planning a destination wedding doesn’t have to strain anyone’s budget. With [smart strategies](/guides/planning-a-destination-wedding), you can manage travel costs so your clients enjoy a beautiful wedding ceremony without unnecessary expenses. Here are some practical tips you can keep in mind to help keep travel costs in check:

### **Choose a Convenient** **Destination Wedding Location**

Discuss with your clients and start by selecting a location that’s easy to reach. Avoid remote destinations that require multiple flights or expensive transfers. Instead, opt for venues close to major airports with direct flights from your client’s or their immediate family’s home country. Locations with good public transportation or shuttle services can also reduce overall travel expenses.

For instance, if you’re planning a wedding for clients from Mexico, a destination like Costa Rica, which has direct flights and affordable shuttle services, could be a more budget-friendly option than a remote island.

#### **Communicate Early with Guests**

Communicate clearly with whoever is on your guest list throughout the planning process. Consider setting up a wedding website or social media group where you can share details about the destination, travel options, and any cost-saving tips. 

A better way is to [**create a trip with SquadTrip**](/). It allows you to create detailed booking pages that include the trip itinerary, trip packages, and any events. It also lets the guests customize their travel experience according to their preferences.

Furthermore, you can encourage guests to book their flights and accommodations early to take advantage of better deals.

#### **Compare Travel Options**

Help your clients by comparing different travel options. Use online tools and apps to find the best deals on flights, car rentals, and trains. Look for group booking discounts, off-season travel deals, or early reservation packages. Consider alternative transportation modes, like buses or ridesharing, which might be more economical.

#### **Maximize Travel Credit Card Points**

Advise your clients on the benefits of using travel credit cards. By paying for wedding-related expenses with a card that offers travel rewards, they can accumulate points that reduce costs for flights, guest accommodation, or even the honeymoon. Remind them to use the card wisely to avoid unnecessary debt.

According to their financial situation, your client could use a travel credit card to pay for their wedding venue deposit, earning enough points to cover part of their honeymoon airfare.

#### **Negotiate with Vendors**

Don’t hesitate to negotiate with vendors for added perks. Ask hotels or resorts if they can include free or discounted airport transfers, or see if you can arrange local transportation for guests.

Let’s say you’re working with a photographer from out of town, and negotiate to have travel fees waived in exchange for booking a full wedding weekend package.

## **Creating** **Wedding Packages** **to Save Costs**

Creating tailored wedding packages is a smart way to keep costs down while ensuring your clients get the most out of their special day. Here’s how you can do it:

-   **Comprehensive** **Wedding Packages****:** Bundle essential services like the ceremony, reception brunch, photography, videography, and entertainment into one package. This not only simplifies planning but also allows you to negotiate better rates with vendors, passing on the savings to your clients.
-   **Flexible Package Options:** Offer packages that cater to different needs:
    -   **Ceremony-Only Package:** For couples who want an intimate event without a reception.
    -   **Wedding Reception****\-Only Package:** For those who have already had a ceremony elsewhere.
    -   **Photography and Videography Package:** For couples who only need professional media services.
-   **Group Travel Packages:** Secure group rates for guests, especially if more than 10 are attending. This can lead to a significant reduction in wedding costs and added perks like exclusive promotions and private events. For example, a room block at a Mexican resort could offer discounts and additional benefits like complimentary excursions for close friends.
