## Why Most Organizers Undervalue Their Trips

If you’ve ever felt unsure about how much to charge, you’re not alone.

Many organizers accidentally undercharge because they:

-   Feel guilty charging what it’s worth
    
-   Forget about hidden costs like tips, transportation, taxes, or comped spots
    
-   Ignore the value of their time, energy, and planning
    
-   Rely on a “gut feeling” rather than a smart pricing strategy
    

The result?

Trips that sell out fast… but leave you with little to no profit.

It doesn’t have to be this way.

## 6 Key Pricing Inputs You Need to Consider

To price your trip properly and protect your earnings, you need to think holistically about every cost and factor.

Here’s what to include:

### 1️⃣ Fixed Costs (Per Person)

These are the expenses you incur for each individual traveler.

-   Hotel rooms
    
-   Meals
    
-   Tickets or excursions
    
-   Travel insurance (if included)
    

Tip: Comped travelers count here — because you’ll still be paying for their lodging, tickets, and meals.

* * *

### 2️⃣ Variable / Group-Level Costs

These are fixed costs for the trip as a whole, which get divided among paying travelers only.

-   Private transportation
    
-   Photographer or videographer
    
-   Welcome gifts
    
-   On-site staff
    

Tip: Do NOT divide these costs by total group size. Comped travelers don’t pay — so the fixed cost needs to be divided among paying guests.

* * *

### 3️⃣ Organizer Fee / Profit Margin

Your time and effort matter.

You should always build in a fair profit margin.

-   Recommended range: 10–30% of total cost
    
-   This covers your planning, client communication, and unexpected time spent.
    

* * *

### 4️⃣ Free / Comped Spots

Hosting a birthday trip? Bringing an assistant?

If anyone is attending for free, those costs need to be absorbed by the paying travelers.

Tip: Forgetting this step is one of the biggest reasons organizers accidentally lose money.

* * *

### 5️⃣ Platform + Transaction Fees

Every payment processor takes a cut.

-   Stripe, credit card fees → ~3–4%
    
-   SquadTrip platform fees (if applicable)
    

Make sure your pricing accounts for these so they don’t eat into your profit.

* * *

### 6️⃣ Unexpected Buffer

Things happen:

-   Vendor price increases
    
-   Last-minute changes
    
-   Emergency expenses
    

Always add a 5–10% buffer to your per-person price to give yourself breathing room.

## Pricing Strategy Examples

Let’s make this practical.

### Example 1: Weekend Getaway for 10 People

-   Per Person Costs: $500
    
-   Fixed Group Costs: $2,000 → $200 per paying traveler
    
-   Subtotal: $700
    
-   Platform Fees + Buffer: +10% → $770
    
-   Organizer Profit (20%): $924 per traveler
    

Price to charge: $925

* * *

### Example 2: Retreat with a Free Host Spot (12 travelers, 1 comped)

-   Fixed costs divided among 11, not 12
    
-   Pricing ensures that host’s spot is fully covered
    

* * *

### Example 3: Festival Trip with Ticket Bundling

-   Per Person Tickets: $250
    
-   Fixed Group Shuttle: $1,000
    
-   Pricing includes convenience & markup for your planning effort
