## What to consider before choosing an alternative

Before deciding on a new tool, take a moment to understand what your agency truly needs. A good platform should match your booking style, team size and complexity of operations.

### – Booking and reservation system

Select a platform that supports the type of products you sell such as single day tours, multi day tours, private trips, custom experiences, add ons and real time availability tracking.

### – Itinerary creation

Some tools offer visual itinerary builders while others provide simple proposal templates. Choose the level of brand presentation your agency requires.

### – Payments and accounting

Look for support for deposits, instalments, multi currency billing, refunds and clear payout reporting.

### – CRM and lead management

If enquiries and follow ups are a big part of your sales cycle, pick a system with a strong CRM to help your team stay organised.

### – Integrations

Your new tool should connect smoothly with your website, payment gateway, accounting tools and marketing software.

### – Pricing and scalability

Growth often means more bookings and more users. Make sure the pricing structure fits your future plans.

### – Support and onboarding

A good software partner should offer training, onboarding assistance and helpful documentation.

## Top 10 RezMagic Alternatives for Travel Agencies

Below are the strongest software options you can consider. Each section includes what the tool does well and who it suits best.

### 1\. SquadTrip

SquadTrip is a modern platform built for [group travel](https://squadtrip.com/guides/how-to-make-group-travel-decisions-without-conflict/), retreats and multi traveller experiences. It combines booking pages, [flexible payment plans](https://squadtrip.com/flexible-payment-options-for-group-trips/), guest dashboards and automated reminders in one simple interface. Agencies can [create professional trip pages](https://squadtrip.com/product/create-stunning-trip-landing-pages-in-5-minutes-with-squadtrip/), collect instalments and track every guest from one dashboard.

**Why it works**  
It is user friendly, fast to set up and great for group travel where payment management usually becomes a challenge.

**Best for**  
Retreat hosts, group trip planners, travel agencies handling multi traveller packages and tour operators selling multi day trips.

**Keep in mind**  
It is built mainly for group trips rather than activity only or single day bookings.

### 2\. WeTravel

WeTravel is widely used for group trips, retreats and multi day tours. It simplifies bookings, payments and guest management. Agencies can collect instalments and track guest details easily.

**Best for**  
Teams that run group tours and need flexible payment handling.

**Keep in mind**  
For complex inventory management you may need additional tools.

**Read more: [6 Best WeTravel Alternatives](https://squadtrip.com/guides/best-wetravel-alternatives/)**

### 3\. Rezdy

Rezdy is popular among day tour and activity providers. It focuses on online bookings and distribution. Agencies can connect to resellers and grow their visibility.

**Best for**  
Businesses selling activities, local tours and short experiences.

**Keep in mind**  
It is less focused on multi day itineraries.

### 4\. TrekkSoft

TrekkSoft is trusted globally by tour and activity operators. It handles bookings, channel management and mobile friendly workflows.

**Best for**  
Agencies with international operations or cross border travellers.

**Keep in mind**  
Smaller agencies may not need every feature it offers.

### 5\. Bókun

Bókun is owned by TripAdvisor and focuses on tours and activities. It offers strong booking tools and partner connections.

**Best for**  
Agencies wanting to expand through marketplaces and distribution.

**Keep in mind**  
Multi day itinerary creation may require manual work.

### 6\. Ezus

Ezus is built for tailor made trips and agencies that design custom itineraries. It supports proposal creation, operations and centralised data management.\\

**Best for**  
DMCs, luxury agencies and custom trip specialists.

**Keep in mind**  
It takes a little more setup time.

### 7\. YouLi

YouLi focuses on group travel, retreats and multi day programs. It supports guest dashboards, payment tracking and group communication.

**Best for**  
Retreat planners, educational trips and multi traveller experiences.

**Keep in mind**  
It is not built for high volume activity bookings.

### 8\. FareHarbor

FareHarbor is widely used for adventure tours, rentals and activities. It offers a strong booking engine that works well with websites.

**Best for**  
Agencies selling adventure activities, tours and rentals.

**Keep in mind**  
Complex, multi day itineraries require additional support.

**Read More: [Top 10 FareHarbor Alternatives](https://squadtrip.com/guides/top-fareharbor-alternatives-for-tour-and-activity-operators/)** 

### 9\. Regiondo

Regiondo is a simple booking system for local tour and activity providers. It is easy to use and quick to set up.

**Best for**  
Small and medium sized operators.

**Keep in mind**  
Advanced automation options are limited.

### 10\. MYT SaaS

MYT SaaS is a complete platform for tour operators. It includes CRM, operations, accounting, supplier management and marketing tools.

**Best for**  
Agencies that need full back office management.

**Keep in mind**  
It may be more than what a small agency requires.

## How to choose the right RezMagic alternative

To narrow your choices, keep these points in mind.

### – Identify your core travel model

Are you selling group trips, custom itineraries, day tours or activities Only then can you match the right tool.

### – Map your daily workflow

List your biggest challenges such as payment tracking, guest communication or supplier coordination.

### – Compare pricing

Check whether the platform charges per booking, per user or as a percentage.

### – Test real scenarios

Run a sample booking, create an itinerary and test how fast your team can learn the platform.
